## Be a user

To understand how to adapt interfaces, learn to work with them in the same mode blind users do. It's simple: turn on VoiceOver on your phone and use the apps.

## How to turn VoiceOver off

Before turning it on, it's important to learn to **turn it off**. In the most difficult situation, Siri will definitely save you: "Hey Siri, turn off
VoiceOver." So you don't run into a disabled Siri, at first turn VoiceOver on only through her.

> Tip: Turn VoiceOver off through Siri

## Ways to turn it on

You can turn on VoiceOver in several ways:

1. Through **Settings** > Accessibility > VoiceOver
2. **Accessibility Shortcut** — triple-press the side button
3. **Back Tap** — double or triple tap on the back of the phone

![Path to VoiceOver through Settings: Settings → Accessibility → VoiceOver](enabling-settings)

## Accessibility Shortcut

VoiceOver settings are in the "Accessibility" section of the phone's settings. Blind users enable a high speech rate — it's more convenient for them that way.

Different accessibility options can be activated by triple-pressing the side button. Look for "Accessibility Shortcut" at the bottom of the "Accessibility" screen.

![Configuring the accessibility shortcut and quick commands.](enabling-shortcut)

## Back Tap

It's convenient to set up turning on VoiceOver with a double (or triple) tap on the back of the phone. This is the fastest way.

![Configuring back tap to turn on VoiceOver.](back-tap)

## Captions for debugging

For debugging, it's convenient not to listen to VoiceOver but to look at the text it reads. By turning on captions, you'll see the description of the current element.

You can also see descriptions through Voice Control — it shows all the elements at once, but that's for a separate chapter.
